# Target Market Research: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Domain:** GTM AI Engineering

## 1. Industry

US-based GTM AI startups whose core product is, or depends on, autonomous or semi-autonomous sales and outbound agents. This is the AI SDR, outbound orchestration, and RevOps AI subset of sales-tech and marketing technology, not "all AI companies" and not "all B2B SaaS."

Concretely, this is the startup tier identified in the career positioning document (11x, Unify, Warmly, Regie.ai, Amplemarket, HockeyStack, Tapistro, Spotlight.ai and their seed-to-Series-B peers), plus the steady stream of newly funded companies entering the same category.

Why this narrowing works: these companies run LLM agents in production as the product itself. That makes reliability, evaluations, cost control, and observability existential rather than optional. It is the precise operational gap this profile fills, so the outreach is relevant on day one instead of generic.

## 2. Company Stage and Size

- **Stage:** seed to Series B.
- **Size:** roughly 15 to 150 employees.
- **Funding recency:** raised within the last 18 months.
- **Maturity band:** post-product, pre-scale. Old enough to have agents live and real reliability pain, young enough to hire fast and engage contractors without a long enterprise procurement cycle.

## 3. Geography

United States. Primary clusters are the San Francisco Bay Area and New York City, plus US remote-first companies.

Rationale: highest density of funded GTM AI startups, US compensation bands, and alignment with the stated goal of US clients and roles.

Operational filter for a remote operator in a non-US time zone: prioritise US companies that are explicitly remote-first and async-friendly. East Coast companies overlap more easily with an India working day than West Coast companies, so they are a higher-yield first tranche for live calls.

## 4. Decision Makers

**Primary decision maker:** CTO, VP of Engineering, or Head of AI/ML. They own the agent reliability problem and control engineering headcount. At seed stage this is often the technical co-founder or CEO.

**Secondary decision maker:** Founding Engineer or Head of Product, who often runs the actual technical evaluation; and the Head of GTM or RevOps, who feels the pain directly when agents misfire and can refer internally.

Responsibility differences worth noting: the CTO or Head of AI decides on platform direction and makes the hire; the Founding Engineer influences and is usually the reviewer of any work sample; the RevOps leader is the internal pain-owner and a warm referral path rather than the buyer.

## 5. Trigger Conditions

A company is worth contacting now if one or more of these signals is present:

- **Recent funding** (seed, Series A, or Series B in the last 12 to 18 months), which means budget and growth pressure.
- **Open roles** for AI/ML engineer, LLMOps or MLOps engineer, platform engineer, forward-deployed engineer, or automation engineer.
- **A newly launched or scaling AI agent or AI SDR product.**
- **Visible agent-reliability pain:** founder or engineer posts about evaluations, hallucinations, agent monitoring, cost or latency, or public incident notes and changelogs about reliability.
- **Rapid headcount growth** visible on the careers page or LinkedIn.
- **An announced automation or agent initiative** or a new AI product line.

These triggers map directly to project evidence: the reliability signals point to Project B (LLMOps observability layer for a GTM agent fleet), and the outbound and discovery signals point to Project A (signal-triggered outbound pipeline). That lets each outreach lead with a specific, relevant artifact rather than a generic pitch.

## 6. Why This ICP

**Domain fit.** GTM AI is the chosen long-term domain. Companies whose entire product is the go-to-market motion automated with agents. Working inside them compounds domain expertise rather than diluting it across unrelated verticals.

**Skills fit.** The profile is a data scientist and AI, ML, LLMOps, and MLOps engineer. This segment's single hardest, least-staffed problem is keeping production agents reliable, cheap, and non-drifting. That is the LLMOps and observability skill set exactly, not an adjacent one. The scoring and prediction skills (lead and account scoring, churn, lifetime value) also map onto the RevOps AI side of the same companies, giving a second point of entry.

**Evidence and projects.** The three project directions from the positioning document are built for this segment. Project B (agent observability and reliability) targets the segment's known operational gap and is the strongest lead artifact. Project A (agentic discovery and outreach) mirrors these companies' own products, showing the candidate understands the thing they sell. Project C (intent scoring) addresses the RevOps side. Each gives a concrete, relevant reason to make contact.

**Access.** This is the practical reason the segment is reachable. Seed-to-Series-B founders and early engineers are active and responsive on LinkedIn and X, teams are small enough that a message reaches a decision maker directly, there is no enterprise procurement wall, and these companies routinely use contractors and forward-deployed engineers. The same maturity band that creates the reliability pain also creates an open door.

**Narrow enough to personalise, large enough to test.** The narrowing is deliberate: one vertical (GTM AI vendors), one stage band (seed to Series B), one geography (US), and a specific pain (agent reliability). That is tight enough that every outreach can name a concrete observation about that company's agent product or a specific open role. Yet the funded US GTM AI startup universe is on the order of a few hundred companies and growing with the funding wave, which is large enough to run a real campaign, iterate on messaging, and collect meaningful reply-rate data rather than a handful of one-off notes.

## ICP Summary

| Field | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | US GTM AI startups building AI SDR, outbound orchestration, or RevOps AI products where LLM agents are the core product |
| **Company stage / size** | Seed to Series B; ~15 to 150 employees; funded within the last 18 months; post-product, pre-scale |
| **Geography** | United States; primary clusters San Francisco Bay Area and New York City; US remote-first companies; East Coast prioritised for time-zone overlap |
| **Primary decision maker** | CTO, VP Engineering, or Head of AI/ML (technical co-founder or CEO at seed stage) |
| **Secondary decision maker** | Founding Engineer or Head of Product (technical reviewer); Head of GTM or RevOps (pain-owner and referral path) |
| **Relevant AI problem** | Production agent reliability: evaluations, observability, cost and latency control, drift detection, and silent-failure handling; secondarily lead and account scoring |
| **Trigger conditions** | Recent funding; open AI/ML, LLMOps, platform, FDE, or automation roles; newly launched or scaling agent product; public agent-reliability pain; rapid headcount growth; announced agent or automation initiative |
| **Exclusion criteria** | Mega-cap incumbents (Salesforce, HubSpot, ZoomInfo) with gated, slow hiring; pre-seed or idea-stage with no product and no budget; non-US companies; companies with no LLM or agent component; pure services or marketing agencies; regulated domain-specialist products (for example clinical healthcare AI) where the candidate lacks vertical depth, unless paired with a domain expert |
