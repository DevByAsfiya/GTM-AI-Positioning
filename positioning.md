# Personal Positioning

**Domain:** GTM AI Engineering

> A target decision maker should understand, in a few seconds, who I help, what problem I solve, and why I am credible.

## Core statement (selected)

> I help **GTM AI startups that ship AI agents** solve **agents that quietly break, drift in quality, and run up cost after launch** using **LLMOps: evaluation, monitoring, and reliability engineering that keeps agents working in production, not just in the demo.**

Broken into the three parts:

- **I help (audience):** US GTM AI startups, roughly Series A to C, whose product depends on AI agents running in production.
- **solve (problem):** AI agents that pass the demo, then quietly break, drift, or overspend once they meet real data and real volume.
- **using (capability):** LLMOps: evaluations, monitoring and observability, cost and latency control, and reliability engineering.

## Three alternative versions considered

| Alternative version | Why it was not selected |
|---|---|
| I help B2B SaaS teams solve unreliable AI features using LLMOps and MLOps. | Audience too broad to target or personalise, and "unreliable AI features" is vaguer than a specific failure a buyer feels. |
| I help AI startups solve getting AI from demo to production using AI engineering. | Leads with building, which appeals to founders, but names no specific pain, and many candidates already claim to "build AI," so it does not differentiate. |
| I help revenue teams solve weak AI outreach using message evaluation and quality control. | Very concrete and ties to AI SDR pain, but narrows to outbound only and cuts off the wider agent-reliability market (support, internal, and other agents). |

## Why the audience and problem are specific enough

**The audience is named, not generic.** Not "AI companies" in general. It is GTM AI startups at Series A to C whose product runs on agents in production, a reachable group with budget and the exact pain. The market research found 135+ such companies in Y Combinator alone, plus funded independents, so the segment is small enough to target and large enough to test.

**The problem is a recognisable failure, not a wish.** Not "make AI better." It is the specific moment an agent passes the demo and then silently degrades, drifts, or burns cost. A buyer already feels this: only about 5 percent of AI pilots show a measurable profit impact, and the 2024 AI SDR slump was blamed on quality. A CTO reads the line and thinks, yes, that is my 2am problem.

## Proof of work that makes the claim credible

- **Domain and market research.** A written domain primer and a sourced market report on GTM AI show command of the space, its workflows, its economics, and exactly where the reliability gap sits.
- **Projects built for this segment.** Three concrete project directions aimed at these companies: an agent observability and reliability layer, a signal-triggered outbound pipeline, and an intent scoring model.
- **A working quality check.** A small tool that scores an AI agent's output for personalisation, spam risk, and quality before it sends, which can be run on a prospect's own product to produce a specific, real result.
- **A skill base that covers both halves of the claim.** Python, Docker and Kubernetes, RAG, evaluation and observability tools (LangSmith, Arize Phoenix, WhyLabs), and scoring models, so I can both build the agent and keep it reliable.

> **Honest note:** the strongest proof today is the research, the working quality check, and the buildable projects. Live production results grow as each project ships, and the positioning is written so nothing claims a track record that does not yet exist.

## Three things that create differentiation

1. **Builds and keeps reliable.** Most people do one: build the agent, or run the ops. Spanning both, agent-building plus the LLMOps reliability layer, is rare, and it is exactly what a small team needs from a single hire.
2. **GTM fluency, not just ML.** I speak the revenue language (ICP, pipeline, deliverability, cost per meeting), so the work targets the failures that actually cost revenue, not generic model metrics.
3. **Reliability and observability focus.** Evals, drift detection, cost and latency control, and silent-failure monitoring, the scarce and underfilled skill that the market's own failures prove is the real gap.

## One-sentence profile headline

> LLMOps engineer who keeps GTM AI agents reliable, cost-controlled, and working in production, not just in the demo.

## Short introduction or outreach paragraph

I'm an AI and ML engineer focused on the unglamorous side of AI agents: keeping them working once they're live. I help GTM AI teams catch the quiet failures, the drift, the cost creep, the demo that breaks on real data, before they show up in the numbers. Building the agent and making it last are the same job to me, and that second half is where most teams need the help.
